How to Re-string a Guitar

This page explains how to re-string and clean an acoustic guitar so that you can get back to cooking up some banger music!

What You Need

  1. Main Tools
    • Wire cutters
    • Needle nose pliers (optional)
    • New guitar strings
    • Micro-fiber cloth
    • Guitar tuner
  2. Preparation
    • Find a flat area where you can lay your guitar. If you can, put something soft (like a towel or cloth) underneath the guitar for extra protection.
    • Loosen all the guitar strings until there is no longer tension on them

Step-by-Step Overview

  1. Once the guitar strings are loosened, take your wire cutters and cut the strings right in the middle
  2. Unwind the cut strings from the tuning pegs, and pull out the string pins with the needle-nose pliers. If you don't have any, try to pull them out with your fingers.
  3. Throw away the old strings
  4. Clean the guitar with the micro-fiber cloth, especially the fretboard.
    • If you have a fretboard conditioner/hydrator, apply a little bit to the cloth, and then gently rub it into the fretboard.
  5. Take out your new pack of strings, and find the low E-string (it should be the thickest string in the pack)
  6. Take the end that has the ball on in and push it into the pin hole for the low E-string. Then put the pin in on top of it and press firmly to make sure the string doesn't come out.
  7. Now thread the other end of the string into the tuning peg for the low E-string.
  8. Hold the string in place as you begin to turn the tuning peg counter-clockwise
    • It can be helpful to hold the string right in front of the tuning peg so that it applies pressure to the string and allows it to coil the right way.
  9. Continue turning the tuning peg until there is noticeable tension on the string (it should have a pitch when you pluck it)
  10. Use your tuner to tune the string to the correct pitch
  11. Repeat these steps with the next string, until you've done all 6
  12. Time to get back to writing some fire music with your polished fretboard and bright new strings!
